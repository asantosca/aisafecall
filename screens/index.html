SafeCall — Demo Screens

Mockups for the caregiver-managed scam-call screening service. Two design languages: the elder app (very large Atkinson Hyperlegible type, high contrast, never more than two actions on a screen) and the caregiver app (standard density, asynchronous management). Personas: Mary (the parent) and Sarah (her daughter, the caregiver). “SafeCall” is a placeholder name.

5 · Stopping or removing SafeCall — both sides

Order matters: forwarding must be switched off before the app is removed, or calls keep flowing to the relay with no app to receive them. The flow enforces that and verifies it with a test call.

🗑 Remove SafeCall completely

Two steps, in this order:

① Turn off call forwarding on Mom’s phone (we do it with one tap there, or dial #21#)

② Delete the app from her phone

6.2 — Android Telecom / ConnectionServiceHigh-priority FCM data message wakes the app into the system call UI. Residual risks (Force Stop, OEM battery killers) are handled at setup (2.3) and by the heartbeat (4.6).

Platform differences that show up in the UX: on iOS the call banner says “SafeCall Audio” (CallKit requirement) and a voicemail alert still arrives even after a force-quit; on Android a Force-Stopped app receives nothing until reopened — which is why the caregiver’s notification path (their own device) is the reliable backstop, and why screen 2.3 (battery exemption) is part of mandatory setup.

Design rules applied to every elder screen: Atkinson Hyperlegible at ≥17 px (body ≥20 px), WCAG-AAA-level contrast, one decision per screen, at most two buttons, no scrolling required for the primary action, spoken audio leads and the screen supplements. Caregiver screens use standard density since the caregiver is the configuring power-user. All names, numbers, and the “SafeCall” brand are placeholders.
